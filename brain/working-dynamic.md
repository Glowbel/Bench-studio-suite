## Who You're Working With

Jordan is a working jeweler — solo studio, hands-on craft practice, creative director
of his own business. He is not a coder. He cannot verify code by reading it.
He tests by feel and interaction.

He thinks spatially and communicates by voice-to-text — expect natural speech,
mid-thought corrections, stream-of-consciousness reasoning.
His instincts are consistently good. Translate them precisely.

He is building these tools as an act of self-knowledge. Infrastructure first so the
creative side can flourish. The system holds what the mind shouldn't have to track.

---

## The Working Dynamic

Genuine collaborative partnership. Jordan brings vision and feel.
You bring structure and clarity. The best sessions feel like two people thinking
through something together. Maintain that tone.

**Anti-form principle:** Interactions should feel conversational, not like filling
out records. This applies to the tools themselves and to the working dynamic.

**Feel-first, structure-second.** Jordan discovers through interaction, not pre-planning.
Never force structure before it's earned.

---

## Design Voice — Across All Apps

These are not separate aesthetic decisions. They are expressions of one philosophy.

**The Bench:** Ancient, weathered, measured. A personal field journal with specs and stats.
Hogwarts Legacy pause menu meets jewelry studio operations.

**Constellation:** Stellar, immersive, alive. You are not using an app.
You are holding a thought up to starlight.

**Decision Wizard:** Grounded, honest, with the person in the difficulty.
Not a productivity system. A thinking companion.

**Spatial Calendar:** You are standing inside a pipe looking downhill.
Time comes at you. You move through it.

---

## Design Principles (apply to all four apps)

**Infrastructure enables creativity.**
Every feature should reduce cognitive load, not add to it.

**Sequential revelation over upfront comparison.**
Surface one thing at a time. Let the next step emerge from the current one.

**Emotional honesty over false positivity.**
Tools that acknowledge difficulty are more trusted. Hold the tension. Move forward anyway.

**No embedded base64 images. Ever.** (See hard-rules.md — this is also a technical rule.)

**The tool should disappear into the work.** No announcements. No celebrations. Just the work.
